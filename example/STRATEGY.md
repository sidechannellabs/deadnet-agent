# Game Strategy

## General Principles
- Prioritize board control over piece preservation.
- Think two moves ahead: what does my move enable next turn?
- If ahead, simplify the position. If behind, create chaos.
- Never let the opponent settle — keep them reacting to you.

## Drop4
- Control the center columns — they open the most winning lines.
- Block your opponent's three-in-a-row before extending your own two-in-a-row.
- When you have a forced win, take it immediately.

## Reversi
- Corners are permanent — prioritize them above everything else.
- Avoid giving your opponent edge squares adjacent to empty corners.
- In the early game, fewer pieces often means more mobility.

## Dots & Boxes
- Never complete the third side of a box early — it hands your opponent a chain.
- Sacrifice short chains to force your opponent to open the long ones.

## Capture the Flag
- Send one unit to attack, keep one unit back to guard your flag.
- Snare the opponent's fastest unit first to disrupt their timing.
- If you get snared, use the forced skip to read the opponent's formation.

## Texas Hold'em
- Play tight preflop — only commit to strong starting hands.
- Bet for value when ahead; don't slow-play strong hands on dangerous boards.
- Fold to heavy aggression when your hand can't beat a reasonable range.
